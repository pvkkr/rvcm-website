Project Overview

What RVCM Does

A low-power, cost-effective remote valve control system using LoRa wireless communication, enabling farmers to open or close water valves by SMS — from anywhere.

📡

Remote Control via SMS

Farmer sends simple ON/OFF SMS commands from any mobile phone to control any valve on the field.

🔋

Ultra-Low Power Design

Deep sleep + MOSFET-gated peripherals keep average power draw near-zero between irrigation cycles.

☀️

Solar Powered

6W solar panel + 12V battery per node. Designed for 3+ years of maintenance-free operation.

📶

LoRa Long-Range Link

Reliable Sub-GHz LoRa wireless across open farm terrain — no Wi-Fi or cellular dependency between nodes.

✅

Valve Position Feedback

Dual limit switches confirm fully open / fully closed state, with SMS acknowledgement back to farmer.

💸

Budget-Friendly

Entire slave node under ₹1,500 using off-the-shelf components — no proprietary or expensive hardware.

Communication Flow

From SMS to Open Valve

A complete end-to-end sequence from farmer's phone to valve actuation and confirmation — all in under 30 seconds.

1

Farmer Sends SMS

Farmer types VALVE1 ON or VALVE2 OFF to the Master Node's GSM number from any mobile phone.

2

Master Node Receives & Parses

GSM module on the Master Node receives the SMS. The ESP32 wakes from deep sleep, reads and parses the command string.

3

LoRa Transmission to Slave

Master activates its LoRa module and transmits the valve command packet to the target Slave Node's address over the LoRa link.

4

Slave Receives & Actuates

Slave Node wakes up its motor driver via MOSFET, runs the DC gear motor to open or close the valve (10–20 seconds).

5

Limit Switch Confirmation

Dual limit switches detect fully open or fully closed valve position, stopping the motor and confirming state to the slave's firmware.

6

Status Acknowledgement

Slave sends status packet back to Master via LoRa. Master optionally sends confirmation SMS to farmer: "Valve 1 OPEN confirmed."

Power Management

Ultra-Low Power Strategy

Designed to run 3+ years on a 6W solar panel through aggressive power gating and deep sleep scheduling.

😴

Deep Sleep Mode

ESP32 sleeps between communication windows. RTC schedules wake-up intervals — near-zero current draw while idle.

⚡

MOSFET Power Gating

LoRa module, motor driver, and GSM are powered via MOSFET switches — fully cut when not in use. No standby leakage.

⚙️

Gear Motor Hold

The DC gear motor holds valve position mechanically after actuation — zero continuous power to maintain open/closed state.

☀️

6W Solar + 12V Battery

Small 6W panel sufficient for low-duty-cycle operation. Buck converter steps 12V → 5V for logic circuits.

Challenges & Mitigation

Engineered for the Field

Every real-world challenge anticipated and addressed by design.

Challenge

Power = Cost

Ultra-low-power design; deep sleep; MOSFET-gated peripherals; small 6W solar panel keeps hardware cost minimal.
Challenge

Long Battery Life

Gear motor needs no continuous power to hold position; RTC scheduling; all modules powered off when idle.
Challenge

Reliable Communication

LoRa in long-range mode with high link budget — well suited for open farm terrain with minimal obstructions.
Challenge

Valve Position Accuracy

Dual limit switches on every actuator confirm fully open / fully closed state before sending acknowledgement.
Challenge

Solar Panel Cost

Achieved by aggressively minimising power requirements — the smaller the consumption, the smaller the panel needed.
Challenge

Ease of Use

Farmer only needs to send a plain SMS — no app, no internet, no smartphone required. Works on any mobile.

Project Timeline

10-Week Implementation Plan

Structured from component procurement to field demonstration.

1
Phase 1 — Weeks 1–2

Component Procurement & Hardware Testing

Source all components, verify quality, run initial bench tests on ESP32, LoRa, GSM, and motor driver modules.

2
Phase 2 — Weeks 3–4

Slave Node Firmware Development

Develop LoRa communication layer and DC gear motor control with limit switch feedback on slave nodes.

3
Phase 3 — Weeks 5–6

Master Node Firmware — GSM + LoRa Gateway

Build SMS command parser, LoRa relay logic, and optional status confirmation SMS back to farmer.

4
Phase 4 — Week 7

Power Management & Deep Sleep Integration

Integrate RTC scheduling, MOSFET power gating, and validate battery life estimates against real power measurements.

5
Phase 5 — Weeks 8–9

Field Testing & Calibration on Farm Site

Deploy all nodes on the actual 3-acre banana farm. Test end-to-end SMS → valve actuation, LoRa range, and solar charging.

6
Phase 6 — Week 10

Documentation, Review & Final Demonstration

Compile technical documentation, conduct final stakeholder demo, and hand over operational system to farm owner.
